Field of Study

Soon after classes started, I realized that Engineering was not for me and that I had more of a love for design and art. This passion struck me to change my major and now I am getting a Bachelors' of Science in Architecture, with a Studio Arts minor and a Sustainability certificate. I learned through this process that sometimes life does not go the way that you planned for it and it is okay to get lost in the weeds of life, it is just a part of it all.

Knowledge, Tools, and Skills

Looking at my experiences, I would say that all throughout highschool and from my first two years of college, I have learned how to design and how to make my design appeal to the specific audience. As an Architect, you have to design for the client and that is something that I want to make sure I do in my future practice. I think it is really important to make sure that a client knows that they have a voice in what they really need and want from my work. As I have grown up, I realized that I am most definitely a maker, I seem to learn best hands on than anything else. I would say that my preferred medium is pencil, to sketch, but my runner up would have to be either Illustrator or AutoCad. Pencil and paper interest me because it is not used in practice much anymore which is kind of sad becuase I think it is beautiful and impressive to be able to show someone your ideas, by drawing it out. Society has lost the pencil and paper idea and has jumped to everything electronically. This is why I like Illustrator and AutoCad, because it is what we now are based out of as architects and they have great features that make your work exceptional, if you know how to use it right. I wish to learn a lot more about animation and how to work that, I also want to learn a little more about graphic design.

Potential Project

Something that I am personally interested in is sustainable Architecture. Sustainable Architecture is designing Architecture with the Earth and its entirety in mind. Some ways to make sustainable Architecture is using solar panels, rainwater reuse systems, green roofs, etc. This is an idea that I could use for this class to make an interactive sustainable house with pop-ups that teach users about this and how they can make small efforts to make their life more 'green'. I think that everyone needs to start getting a greener lifestyle and making a good impact on the world. If we do not start now, what is our world going to look like in 10 years? The main problem about this is that people are not aware of what they are doing and their impact. We need to start teaching, rather than criticizing.
